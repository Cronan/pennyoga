Your first class

What happens when you join a gentle yoga session with me

  • Arrive a few minutes early

    Settle in with your mat and meet the group at your own pace. No need to introduce yourself if you’d rather not.

  • Wear whatever feels comfortable

    No special yoga kit is needed. Loose, comfortable clothes you can move in are perfect. Bare feet or socks, whichever you prefer.

  • Modifications for every movement

    If a pose doesn’t suit your body today, I’ll offer an alternative. You’ll always work within your own range of movement.

  • We end with a proper rest

    Every class finishes with a guided relaxation, usually ten to fifteen minutes. Bring a blanket if you have one — you’ll want it.

Common Questions

Are the classes suitable for beginners or people with limited mobility?
Absolutely. My gentle yoga classes are designed for all levels, including complete beginners and those with reduced mobility. Whether you are managing arthritis, recovering from an injury, or simply feeling stiff, I offer modifications throughout so everyone can work within their own comfortable range of movement.
What should I expect at a gentle yoga class in Berkhamsted or Hemel Hempstead?
Each session is 75 minutes of gentle Hatha Yoga, combining breathwork, simple postures, and relaxation. The atmosphere is friendly and unhurried. Just bring a mat if you have one, wear comfortable clothing, and arrive a few minutes early.
Do I need to book in advance?
Yes. Face-to-face classes run as a block booking. If you are new, I offer a one-off trial session so you can try a class before committing to a block. Get in touch to arrange your trial.
What qualifications does Penny have?
I qualified as a British Wheel of Yoga (BWY) teacher in 2004, after ten years as a yoga student. BWY training is a long, demanding qualification with rigorous entrance requirements, and is nationally recognised. I hold current first aid certification and continue annual professional development.
How much do classes cost?
Face-to-face classes are £10–£12 per session depending on the venue and are booked as a block. The Wednesday online class via Zoom is £9 per session.
What do I need to bring to a face-to-face class?
Please bring your own yoga mat and wear loose, comfortable clothing that lets you move freely. A blanket is useful for the relaxation at the end of the session. Blocks and straps can be helpful but are not essential, especially for your first few classes.
How do the online Wednesday classes work?
The Wednesday evening class is a live, interactive session held via Zoom. Once you book, I will send you the link to join. You practise from home on your own mat and I give guidance throughout, just as in a face-to-face class. You will need the Zoom app installed on your device before your first session.
